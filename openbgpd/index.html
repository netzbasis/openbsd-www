OpenBGPD is a FREE implementation of the Border Gateway Protocol, Version 4. It allows ordinary machines to be used as routers exchanging routes with other systems speaking the BGP protocol.

Started out of dissatisfaction with other implementations, OpenBGPD nowadays is a fairly complete BGP implementation, powering many sites. Users often praise its ease of use and high performance, as well as its reliability.

OpenBGPD's companions, ospfd(8), ospf6d(8), ripd(8), and dvmrpd(8) add support for the respective protocols. ldpd(8) and mpe(4) add MPLS support.

OpenBGPD is primarily developed by Henning Brauer and Claudio Jeker. All these daemons are part of the OpenBSD Project. The software is freely usable and re-usable by everyone under a BSD license.

The OpenBSD project sells CDs, T-shirts and posters. Sales of these items help to fund development.
